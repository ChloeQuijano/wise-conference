Competitions

The WISE National Conference (WISE NC) competitions provide an opportunity for participants from various backgrounds to apply knowledge learned in class and solve real-world problems, working collaboratively as a team. We have expanded our competitions as we take advantage of our virtual platform to include as many contestants from various fields as possible. By having diverse topics that have both scientific and ethical implications, we hope our contestants can propose innovative solutions that are not only technologically sound but also encompass social considerations. This year, we're offering 3 different competitions: Healthcare Case Competition and Pitch, 3 Minute Thesis and an Engineering Case Competition!

Professor Geci Karuri-Sebina

Geci is an urban scholar-practitioner based in Johannesburg working in the intersection between people, place and technological change. She is a Visiting Associate Professor at the University of Witwatersrand’s School of Governance where she is hosting the Civic Tech Innovation Network. She is also associated with the University of Cape Town’s African Centre for Cities, South African Cities Network, a learning network of the country’s metropolitan municipalities, and Singularity University’s Global Faculty on the future of cities and governance. Geci holds Bachelors’ degrees in Computer Science and Sociology (Iowa); Masters’ degrees in Urban Planning and Architecture from UCLA (Los Angeles); and a PhD from the University of Witwatersrand (Joburg). Geci’s work is mainly in Africa and the global south. She has a diverse background, spanning a range of foresight, policy, innovation and practice topics, and has worked extensively in R&D, government and civic organisations. She currently serves as the Vice-Chair of AfricaLICS (the community of innovation scholars in Africa) and Regional Advisor of the Africa Innovation Summit.

Dr. Erin Vogel

As a social psychologist, I’m fascinated by the roles we all play in each other’s lives and well-being, and how technology influences those roles– for better and for worse. My research has explored questions such as: 1) What are the benefits of social media use and how can social media help us improve our health?, 2) How do young people’s social environments, both online and offline, influence their use of e-cigarettes (also known as vaping)?, and 3) To what extent do we compare ourselves to others on social media, and how do those comparisons affect us?

I am currently a Senior Research Associate at the University of Southern California Keck School of Medicine in Los Angeles, California, USA. My research interests include youth tobacco use, social media and digital health, and tobacco use disparities in the LGBTQ+ community. I completed my PhD in Experimental Psychology, with a concentration in Social Psychology, at the University of Toledo (Toledo, Ohio, USA). Prior to joining USC in 2021, I completed postdoctoral fellowships at the University of California, San Francisco and Stanford University.

In addition to my research, I have taught several classes at the undergraduate and graduate levels. Most recently, I taught a graduate seminar on Social Media, Health, and Well-Being at Stanford University. I find mentoring to be among the most rewarding aspects of an academic career, and I enjoy talking with students at all levels about their interests and goals. I am optimistic that we can make academia a more inclusive environment for everyone.

Dr. Imogen R Coe

Dr. Imogen R. Coe is a professor of Chemistry and Biology and former founding dean of the Faculty of Science at X (Ryerson) University in Toronto. She is also an affiliate scientist at St. Michael’s Hospital, Toronto, where her research group studies drug transporters. In addition to being an academic scientist, Dr. Coe is one of Canada's leading advocates for organizational change towards inclusive excellence in research, particularly in science and medicine. She runs a consulting company providing advice and training on inclusive excellence and has advised federal and national funding agencies on how to integrate equity, diversity and inclusion principles into diverse research cultures. She has published on inclusive leadership, misogyny in science and the need for intentional policy around EDI-infused organizational culture in diverse venues such as The Lancet and the Globe and Mail. She is much in demand as a speaker and panelist and has received numerous awards for her advocacy work.

Dr. Emily Rickman

I am a research fellow for the European Space Agency stationed at the Space Telescope Science Institute in Baltimore, USA, home to the Hubble Space Telescope and the upcoming James Webb Space Telescope. I hunt for planets outside of our Solar System -- exoplanets -- using some of the world's largest ground- and space-based telescopes to try and understand their formation and evolution. I am also a member of the commissioning team for the upcoming launch of the James Webb Space Telescope.

I studied an integrated Masters in Physics and Astrophysics at the University of Sheffield, UK, including spending one year at the Australian National University, Australia. I then did my PhD in Astronomy and Astrophysics at the University of Geneva, Switzerland before moving to the states.

As a woman in science, who is also a member for the LGBTQ+ community, I strive for visibility of underrepresented and marginalized folks in STEM, including participating in several diversity-orientated committees, forums and panels, as well as acting as a mentor and role model for those wanting to follow a STEM career path. I co-founded the 'diversity in science' committee at Geneva Observatory; I currently sit on the 'women in astronomy' forum at the Space Telescope Science Institute, and I founded and curate the @LGBTQIAinAstro Twitter account.

Dr. Alexandra King

Dr. Alexandra King, MD, FRCPC, is an Internal Medicine Specialist with a focus on HIV/AIDS, Hepatitis C (HCV) and HIV/HCV co-infections. Alexandra is a Nipissing First Nations woman (Ontario, Canada). Alexandra is the inaugural Cameco Chair in Indigenous Health and Wellness at the University of Saskatchewan (Saskatchewan, Canada), and the Co-Chair of International Group on Indigenous Health Measurement (IGIHM)’s working group on Indigenous wellness. She works with Indigenous communities and relevant stakeholders to understand the health and wellness needs of First Nations and Métis peoples in Saskatchewan and the structural changes that are needed for improved Indigenous health outcomes.

Dr. Isabel Bishop

I get people involved in science. Specifically, I help run a global 'citizen science' project called 'FreshWater Watch.' Nearly 15,000 people from 32 countries have helped us to collect data on the health of our freshwater ecosystems, and my job involves making sure that the data and information they contribute is used as part of conservation efforts around the world.

I studied Geography at university and fell in love with the subject. As a lifelong open water swimmer, I was particularly drawn to the elements of the course that focussed on freshwater conservation. I've subsequently spent most of my career focussed on the interactions between humans and water. Fresh water is vital for our lives, livelihoods, and happiness, but it is incredibly vulnerable to climate change and human pressures. Most people now are aware of climate change and biodiversity loss, but far fewer know about the water crisis. I'm working hard to change that.

Oyinkansola Romiluyi

Oyinkansola Romiluyi is a PhD Candidate in Chemical Engineering at UC Berkeley researching the electrochemical conversion of carbon-dioxide to fuels and chemicals. She has a Masters from Columbia University and an Honours Bachelors with a Sustainable Energy minor from the University of Toronto, St. George, both in Chemical Engineering. Her current and future interests lie in industrial electrosynthesis, machine learning, automation, and how the cross between these fields can lead to accelerated innovation and commercialization in the frontiers of science, engineering, and applied research in humanity’s path towards addressing green-house gas emissions and climate change.

Dr. M. Dolores Cimini

Dr. M. Dolores Cimini is a New York State licensed psychologist and Director of the Center for Behavioral Health Promotion and Applied Research at the University at Albany, SUNY. She is also the Director of the nationally recognized Middle Earth Peer Assistance Program. Dr. Cimini has led comprehensive efforts in research-to-practice translation at the University at Albany for the past 30 years with over $10 Million in support from the National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism (NIAAA), National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA), Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA), U.S. Department of Education, U.S. Department of Justice, and New York State Office of Addiction Services and Supports. The screening and brief intervention program developed by Dr. Cimini, the STEPS Comprehensive Alcohol Screening and Brief Intervention Program, has earned 13 national awards for best practices and innovation in behavioral health care. Dr. Cimini has co-edited two books, including a volume focused on college student health and well-being entitled Promoting Behavioral Health and reducing Risk Among College Students: A Comprehensive Approach (2018).

Dr. Cimini has served as a member of the National Advisory Council of the Center for Substance Abuse Prevention, Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration from 2015-2019 and is currently a member of the Commission on Accreditation of the American Psychological Association. She served on the governance board of the American Psychological Association, where she is the past Chair of the Board for the Advancement of Psychology in the Public Interest; in this role, she has had leadership for reviewing and disseminating APA’s practice standards focused on serving diverse and underrepresented groups and the addressing of issues related to psychology and social justice.

In 2012, Dr. Cimini was recognized by President Barack Obama and the White House for her work in promoting access to Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) for women with disabilities and for serving as a role model as a person with a disability. In 2014, she received a Presidential Citation from the American Psychological Association for her contributions to the psychology field and her advocacy on behalf of persons with disabilities in the STEM sciences. In 2018, Dr. Cimini earned a Presidential Award for Excellence in Science, Mathematics, and Engineering Mentoring (PAESMEM).

Dr. Janet Ngu

Dr. Janet Ngu is an attending cardiac surgeon at Vancouver General Hospital. Dr. Ngu earned her M.D. and M.Sc. in Cardiovascular Sciences from the University of Calgary. She went on to pursue Cardiac Surgery Residency training at the University of Ottawa Heart Institute. While fulfilling her residency clinical duties, she completed the Global Clinical Scholar Research Training program from Harvard Medical School. After completing her residency in 2019, Dr. Ngu received further fellowship training at Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago with a special focus in complex valve surgeries, heart failure, and mechanical circulatory support. She went on to complete a second fellowship in robotic and minimally invasive cardiac surgery at the Cleveland Clinic. After completing two of the most high-profile fellowships in the USA, Dr. Ngu returned to Canada with her expertise and joined the faculty of University of British Columbia. Dr. Ngu has an aptitude for both translational and clinical research, with over 30 peer-reviewed publications and book chapters to date. She has received multiple research awards both in Canada and internationally. Dr. Ngu has a keen academic interest in the cardiac surgery outcomes for female patients, who were generally underrepresented in the previous research.

Sri Subramanian

Sri is currently the Head of Product for Cloud Identity and Access Management at Google. She has a long history of building and growing innovative and market leading security products across a number of markets (Secure Web Gateway, Cloud Firewall, VPN, IAM, Protocol and OS security and more). She is an advisor to various early stage startups and actively consults with analysts and investors on industry trends. She is featured in Cyber Magazine's Top 10 Female Leaders in Cybersecurity, Software Report's Top 25 Women in Cybersecurity for 2020, and Cyber Defense Awards' Top 100 Women in Cybersecurity for 2020.
